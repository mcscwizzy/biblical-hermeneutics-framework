## Purpose

This module helps you approach 1 Corinthians as Paul's response to a divided,
gifted, and conflicted church in a cosmopolitan Greco-Roman city — addressing one
concrete problem after another. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it
tells you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already
loaded. It does not tell you what 1 Corinthians means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in 1 Corinthians, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and the
Greco-Roman social world of [[context.greco-roman-world]] and
[[context.roman-empire]].

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) structured largely as a series of responses to
**reports** and **questions** from the church ("now concerning…"). Watch for
**rhetorical** argument, **quoted slogans** Paul takes up and qualifies, and
embedded units such as the **love** passage and the **resurrection** argument.

## Historical anchors

**Corinth** — a status-conscious Roman colony and trade city
([[context.roman-empire]], [[context.greco-roman-world]]) where **honor, shame,
patronage, and social rank** ([[context.honor-shame]]) shaped the conflicts (over
leaders, lawsuits, meals, and gifts). Pauline authorship is the scholarly
consensus; the situation must be reconstructed from Paul's side of the
correspondence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **"now concerning"** topic markers, **quoted Corinthian slogans** (and
Paul's qualification of them), **rhetorical questions**, the use of **Old Testament**
example and citation (see [[core.intertextuality]]), the structured set pieces (love;
resurrection), and **Greek** wording that bears on contested phrases
(see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in a given passage;
look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What concrete situation or question is Paul addressing here, so far as it can be reconstructed?
- Is Paul stating his own view, or quoting and qualifying a Corinthian slogan?
- How do honor, status, and patronage in Corinth illuminate the conflict?
- How does this passage fit Paul's larger argument in the letter?
- What does a debated term or construction mean in context, and what are the options?
- How is Paul using earlier Scripture here?

## Common misreadings

- Reading a verse apart from the specific Corinthian situation Paul answers.
- Mistaking a quoted Corinthian slogan for Paul's own assertion.
- Lifting the set pieces (love, resurrection) out of their argumentative setting.
- Settling debated, culturally embedded passages by appeal to a tradition's doctrine rather than the argument and evidence.

## Handling uncertainty

The Corinthian situation behind several passages, and the meaning of culturally
specific instructions, are debated and read differently across traditions. Present
the major responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
