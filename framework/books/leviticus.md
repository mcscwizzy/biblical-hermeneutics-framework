## Purpose

This module helps you approach Leviticus as ritual and covenant instruction about
how a holy God may be approached and how a people are to live in his presence. It
is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask,
assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It does not tell you what
Leviticus means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Leviticus, on top of [[genre.law]] — the book is
predominantly legal and ritual instruction set within the Sinai narrative.

## Genre signals

Predominantly **law** ([[genre.law]]), in its ritual and priestly mode:
sacrifice, priesthood, purity and impurity, festivals, and the so-called Holiness
material. A thin **narrative** thread frames it (e.g., the events of the
priesthood's first days). Distinguish ritual instruction, apodictic command, and
case law as you read.

## Historical anchors

The world of **temple and sacrifice** ([[context.temple]]) — how sacred space,
priesthood, and offerings were understood — and the **covenant** relationship the
instruction serves ([[context.covenant]]), against the wider **ancient Near East**
([[context.ancient-near-east]]), where comparable ritual and purity systems
existed. Note both likeness and distinctiveness without assuming borrowing
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for **ritual structure and repetition** (recurring formulae for offerings
and rites), **purity/impurity and holiness gradations**, **symbolic** uses of
blood, fire, and space, **refrains** ("I am the LORD," "be holy"), structured
**festival calendars**, and the heavy later **reuse** of Levitical categories
elsewhere in Scripture (see [[core.intertextuality]]). Do not assume every feature
governs a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What does this rite or rule assume about holiness, purity, and approaching God?
- What is the stated purpose or effect of the offering, rite, or boundary described?
- Is the passage prescribing ritual, defining status, or commanding conduct?
- What would an ancient worshipper have understood that a modern reader might miss?
- How are these categories taken up or transformed later in Scripture?
- How does the question of ongoing application bear on this passage, and what approaches exist?

## Common misreadings

- Dismissing ritual law as arbitrary instead of asking its function and logic.
- Importing modern hygiene, science, or ethics as the explanation before observing the text.
- Allegorizing ritual details into a code while missing their stated purpose.
- Settling the contested question of a law's ongoing application by assumption rather than careful, stated reasoning.

## Handling uncertainty

The rationale of specific purity and sacrificial categories, the relation to ANE
ritual, the book's composition, and how (or whether) its provisions apply to later
readers are debated and tradition-linked. Present the major approaches, separate
observation from conclusion, and label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave the conclusion to the reader.
