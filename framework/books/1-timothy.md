## Purpose

This module helps you approach 1 Timothy, one of the Pastoral Epistles, as
instruction for ordering a church's life and confronting false teaching. It is a
hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask, assuming
Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It does not tell you what 1 Timothy
means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in 1 Timothy, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and the
household and civic world of [[context.greco-roman-world]] and [[context.honor-shame]].

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) in the **Pastoral** mode — addressed to a
delegate rather than a congregation — combining instruction on **church order**,
warnings against **false teaching**, **qualification lists** for leaders, **household**
instruction, and compact creedal or hymnic fragments. Read these instructional
forms on their own terms.

## Historical anchors

The **Greco-Roman household and civic world** ([[context.greco-roman-world]],
[[context.honor-shame]]) shaping expectations of leaders, women, slaves, and
respectability, with false teaching in **Ephesus** as the foil. Whether Paul himself
wrote the Pastorals is debated among scholars; present the major approaches and weigh
the evidence rather than assuming a verdict (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for **qualification lists** (overseers, deacons), **household-management**
language applied to the church, instructions concerning **women, widows, elders, and
slaves**, warnings against **myths and disputes**, **trustworthy sayings** and
creedal fragments, and echoes of **Old Testament** (see [[core.intertextuality]]);
**Greek** wording can bear on contested phrases (see [[language.greek]]). Do not
assume every feature is present in a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What church-order or false-teaching problem is this instruction addressing?
- How do Greco-Roman household and honor expectations illuminate the instruction?
- How much is shaped by the specific Ephesian situation, and how widely was it meant to apply — a question to weigh, not assume?
- What does a debated, culturally embedded passage (e.g., instructions about women) mean in context, and what major approaches exist?
- How does a creedal or hymnic fragment function in its setting?

## Common misreadings

- Reading culturally embedded instructions as timeless without weighing the situation and the range of approaches.
- Treating the qualification lists as exhaustive job descriptions rather than character sketches in their setting.
- Settling the authorship question by assumption rather than weighing the evidence.
- Settling contested passages by appeal to a tradition's doctrine rather than the text and its setting.

## Handling uncertainty

The Pastorals' authorship, the Ephesian situation, and the application of several
culturally specific instructions are debated and read very differently across
traditions. Present the major responsible interpretations and their basis, label
confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
