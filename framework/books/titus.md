## Purpose

This module helps you approach Titus, a Pastoral Epistle, as concise instruction for
ordering churches on Crete — appointing leaders, countering false teaching, and
linking sound teaching to godly conduct. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a
commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context
are already loaded. It does not tell you what Titus means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Titus, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and the
household and civic world of [[context.greco-roman-world]] and [[context.honor-shame]].

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) in the **Pastoral** mode — addressed to a
delegate — combining **leadership qualifications**, instruction for **household
groups**, warnings against **false teachers**, and compact **doctrinal summaries**
that ground conduct in the message. Read these instructional forms on their own
terms.

## Historical anchors

The **Greco-Roman household and civic world** ([[context.greco-roman-world]],
[[context.honor-shame]]) of **Crete**, including its reputation in the cited proverb
and the honor expectations behind instructions to various groups. Whether Paul
himself wrote the Pastorals is debated among scholars; present the major approaches
and weigh the evidence rather than assuming a verdict (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **qualification list** for elders/overseers, the cited **Cretan
proverb**, instruction to **age and status groups** (older men and women, younger
people, slaves), the recurring link between **"sound teaching" and good works**,
compact **doctrinal summaries** of grace, and echoes of **Old Testament**
(see [[core.intertextuality]]); **Greek** wording can bear on meaning
(see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in a given passage;
look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What church-order or false-teaching problem on Crete is this instruction addressing?
- How do Greco-Roman household and honor expectations illuminate the instruction to each group?
- How widely was a given instruction meant to apply beyond its setting — a question to weigh, not assume?
- How do the doctrinal summaries function to ground the ethical instruction?
- How does the citation of the Cretan proverb work rhetorically?

## Common misreadings

- Reading culturally embedded household instructions as timeless without weighing the setting and the range of approaches.
- Treating the qualification list as an exhaustive job description rather than a character sketch in its setting.
- Settling the authorship question by assumption rather than weighing the evidence.
- Lifting the doctrinal summaries out of their conduct-shaping purpose.

## Handling uncertainty

The Pastorals' authorship, the Cretan situation, and the application of several
culturally specific instructions are debated and read differently across traditions.
Present the major responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
