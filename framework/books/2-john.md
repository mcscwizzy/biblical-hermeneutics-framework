## Purpose

This module helps you approach 2 John as a brief, true-to-form letter warning a
community about traveling teachers and the limits of hospitality, in the Johannine
idiom of truth and love. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you
what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It does
not tell you what 2 John means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in 2 John, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and the
Johannine themes it shares with 1 John and the Gospel of John.

## Genre signals

A genuine short **letter** ([[genre.epistle]]) — among the briefest in the New
Testament — with a normal epistolary opening and closing, addressed to "the elect
lady and her children" (most read as a church and its members). Read it as a real
occasional letter with a single main concern.

## Historical anchors

The world of **traveling teachers and household hospitality** in the early churches
([[context.greco-roman-world]]) — where receiving a teacher into one's house meant
endorsing and supporting them — lies behind the warning. The identity of the "elect
lady" and the situation are debated; hold them with appropriate confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **epistolary frame** (sender, addressee, greeting, closing), the
**truth-and-love** pairing, the **warning** against those who do not "abide in the
teaching," the instruction on **hospitality** to such teachers, and shared
**Johannine** vocabulary (see [[core.intertextuality]]); **Greek** wording can bear
on meaning (see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in this
short letter; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What concrete situation with traveling teachers is the letter addressing?
- What did offering or withholding hospitality mean in this setting?
- How do "truth" and "love" function together in the letter's concern?
- Who are "the elect lady and her children," and how does that reading affect interpretation?
- How does the language connect to 1 John and the Gospel of John?

## Common misreadings

- Reading the instruction on hospitality as a timeless rule apart from its specific situation of endorsing traveling teachers.
- Pressing the brief letter for more doctrinal detail than it offers.
- Treating "the elect lady" woodenly without weighing the likely corporate reading.
- Detaching the letter from the wider Johannine writings it echoes.

## Handling uncertainty

The identity of the addressee, the precise situation, and the relationship to the
other Johannine writings are debated. Present the major responsible interpretations
and their basis, label confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the
conclusion to the reader.
