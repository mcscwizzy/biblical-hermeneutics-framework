## Purpose

This module helps you approach 2 Timothy, a Pastoral Epistle, as a personal,
testament-like charge to endure suffering and guard the message, set against the
backdrop of imprisonment. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells
you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It
does not tell you what 2 Timothy means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in 2 Timothy, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and its
Greco-Roman setting ([[context.greco-roman-world]], [[context.roman-empire]]).

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) in the **Pastoral** mode but markedly **personal**
and **testamentary** — a charge from an imprisoned mentor to a delegate. It combines
**exhortation to endurance**, warnings about **false teachers** and the **last
days**, and personal notes. Read its personal and hortatory character together.

## Historical anchors

The **Greco-Roman** world ([[context.greco-roman-world]], [[context.roman-empire]])
of itinerant teaching, **imprisonment**, and trial. Whether Paul himself wrote the
Pastorals is debated among scholars; present the major approaches and weigh the
evidence rather than assuming a verdict (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **testamentary** tone (passing on a charge), **endurance and suffering**
imagery (soldier, athlete, farmer), the call to **guard the deposit** and continue in
what was learned, the statement about **Scripture** being God-breathed and useful,
warnings about the **last days**, **personal notes** and names, and echoes of **Old
Testament** (see [[core.intertextuality]]); **Greek** wording can bear on meaning
(see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in a given passage;
look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- How does this passage function as a charge to endure and to guard the message?
- How does the testamentary, personal tone shape the reading?
- What is the situation of suffering or opposition behind the passage?
- How does a statement such as the one about Scripture function in its immediate argument?
- What major approaches exist to the Pastorals' authorship, and what is the evidence?

## Common misreadings

- Lifting famous lines (e.g., about Scripture, or "fought the good fight") out of their charge-to-endure context.
- Reading the "last days" warnings as a detached timetable.
- Settling the authorship question by assumption rather than weighing the evidence.
- Treating the personal notes as incidental rather than part of the letter's purpose.

## Handling uncertainty

The Pastorals' authorship, the circumstances of the imprisonment, and some
references are debated and read differently across traditions. Present the major
responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
