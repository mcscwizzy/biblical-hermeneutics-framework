## Purpose

This module helps you approach 1 John as a pastoral writing in the Johannine idiom,
giving readers tests of genuine faith and love in the wake of a community split. It
is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask,
assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It does not tell you what
1 John means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in 1 John, on top of [[genre.epistle]] — read in the
distinctive Johannine style it shares with the Gospel of John.

## Genre signals

Though counted among the epistles ([[genre.epistle]]), 1 John lacks a normal letter
opening and closing; it reads more like a **sermon or circular exhortation**. It
proceeds by **spiral repetition** rather than linear argument, returning to its
themes from new angles. Read it cyclically, not as a step-by-step treatise.

## Historical anchors

A **schism** in the community lies behind the letter — people who "went out" — and
the **Greco-Roman** intellectual setting ([[context.greco-roman-world]]) and
**Second Temple Jewish** background ([[context.second-temple-judaism]]) inform its
concerns about who Jesus is and what claims are true. The exact nature of the
opponents is reconstructed and debated (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **spiral/cyclical** structure, sharp **antitheses** (light/darkness,
love/hate, truth/lie, from God / from the world), the **"tests"** of authentic faith
(belief, love, obedience), the **"we/you/they"** distinctions framing the schism, key
repeated terms, and shared **Johannine** language echoing the Gospel of John
(see [[core.intertextuality]]); **Greek** wording can bear on meaning
(see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in a given passage;
look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- How does this passage function as a "test" or reassurance for the readers after the split?
- How does the spiral structure return to and develop a theme here?
- What can be discerned of the opponents' claims, and how cautiously?
- How do the sharp antitheses shape the passage without being pressed into a rigid system?
- How does the language echo the Gospel of John?

## Common misreadings

- Reading 1 John as a linear argument and missing its spiral, repetitive movement.
- Pressing its absolute-sounding statements (e.g., about sin) without weighing how they function pastorally and against the opponents.
- Over-reconstructing the opponents and reading the whole letter through that reconstruction.
- Treating the antitheses as a metaphysical system rather than pastoral contrasts.

## Handling uncertainty

The nature of the opponents, the relationship to the Gospel of John, and the force of
some absolute statements are debated and read differently across traditions. Present
the major responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
