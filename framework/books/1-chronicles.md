## Purpose

This module helps you approach 1 Chronicles as a retelling of Israel's story for
the post-exilic community — genealogies of identity, then David's reign focused on
worship and the temple. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells
you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded.
It does not tell you what 1 Chronicles means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in 1 Chronicles, on top of [[genre.narrative]] and
the post-exilic perspective in [[context.exile-and-restoration]] — and, because it
retells the monarchy, [[context.israelite-monarchy]].

## Genre signals

A mix of extensive **genealogies and lists** and **narrative** ([[genre.narrative]])
retelling David's reign. The genealogies are not filler but a statement of
identity and continuity; read lists by their own conventions and the narrative as
shaped, selective storytelling.

## Historical anchors

Written for the **post-exilic / restoration** community ([[context.exile-and-restoration]])
looking back on the **monarchy** ([[context.israelite-monarchy]]), with a strong
focus on the **temple** and its worship ([[context.temple]]). The Chronicler
selects and reshapes earlier material for that community's situation. Date and
sources are debated; hold them with appropriate confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the long **genealogies** establishing identity and continuity, **selective
retelling** that reshapes the Samuel–Kings material (see [[core.intertextuality]]),
emphasis on **David's preparations for the temple** and the ordering of worship,
recurring themes (seeking the LORD, the priesthood and Levites), and **speeches**
and prayers that carry the message. Do not assume every feature is present in a
given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What is the function of this genealogy or list for a community re-establishing its identity?
- How does the Chronicler's version differ from the parallel in Samuel–Kings, and what might the differences emphasize?
- Is the passage reporting events or commending a response?
- Why is worship and the temple given such prominence here?
- What would the post-exilic audience have heard in this material that a modern reader might miss?
- How does this passage reuse and reshape earlier Scripture?

## Common misreadings

- Skipping the genealogies as filler rather than asking their purpose for the audience.
- Reading Chronicles as a simple duplicate of Samuel–Kings and missing its distinct emphases.
- Treating the differences from Samuel–Kings as mere error rather than purposeful selection — a question to weigh, not assume.
- Treating narrated actions as automatically endorsed or prescribed.

## Handling uncertainty

The date, sources, and compositional aims of Chronicles, and the reasons behind
specific differences from Samuel–Kings, are debated. Present the range, distinguish
observation from reconstruction, and label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave conclusions to the reader.
