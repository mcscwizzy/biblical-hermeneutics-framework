## Purpose

This module helps you approach Zephaniah as a prophecy of the day of the LORD —
sweeping judgment on Judah and the nations, narrowing to a humble remnant and a
closing promise of joy. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you
what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It
does not tell you what Zephaniah means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Zephaniah, on top of [[genre.prophecy]] and its
Hebrew **poetry** ([[genre.poetry]]).

## Genre signals

Prophetic **poetry** ([[genre.poetry]], [[genre.prophecy]]) organized around the
**day of the LORD**: judgment oracles against **Judah**, **oracles against the
nations**, and a final turn to **restoration**. Identify whether a unit announces
judgment, indicts a nation, or promises hope.

## Historical anchors

**Judah** in the seventh century, traditionally set in the reign of Josiah, before
or around his reforms, within the **monarchy** period ([[context.israelite-monarchy]]),
judged against the **covenant** standard ([[context.covenant]]). Hold the
historical details with appropriate confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the pervasive **day of the LORD** motif (echoed widely in later tradition),
the **cosmic-to-local** sweep of judgment, **oracles against the nations**, the
theme of a humble **remnant** that "seeks the LORD," the closing **song of joy**,
and reuse of shared prophetic language (see [[core.intertextuality]]). Do not
assume every feature is present in a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- Is this passage judgment on Judah, an oracle against a nation, or a promise of restoration?
- How does the day-of-the-LORD theme frame the passage?
- Which images are figurative, and what did they evoke?
- How does the book move from sweeping judgment to the remnant and joy?
- What would a seventh-century Judahite audience have understood that a modern reader might miss?

## Common misreadings

- Reading the day-of-the-LORD language as a detached end-times timetable rather than within the book's message.
- Isolating the closing joy from the judgment that precedes it, or the reverse.
- Flattening the poetry into flat prose.
- Settling the book's date and setting by assumption rather than observation.

## Handling uncertainty

The book's date and setting, the extent of editing, and the scope of its judgment
language are debated. Present the range, distinguish observation from
reconstruction, and label confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave
conclusions to the reader.
