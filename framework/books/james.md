## Purpose

This module helps you approach James as a letter steeped in the wisdom tradition —
practical, pointed exhortation on faith that shows itself in conduct, the tongue,
wealth and poverty, and patience. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it
tells you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already
loaded. It does not tell you what James means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in James, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and the
**wisdom** sensibility that pervades it ([[genre.wisdom]]).

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) with a diaspora-letter opening, but its body
reads like **wisdom** ([[genre.wisdom]]) — loosely linked exhortations, **aphorisms**,
vivid **illustrations**, and **diatribe**-style address. It echoes Jesus's teaching
(especially the Sermon on the Mount) without quoting it as such. Read units as
wisdom exhortation rather than a single linear argument.

## Historical anchors

The **wisdom tradition** behind its form, the **Second Temple Jewish** matrix
([[context.second-temple-judaism]]) of its diaspora address and concerns, and the
**honor-shame** dynamics ([[context.honor-shame]]) behind its treatment of partiality,
wealth, poverty, and speech. Authorship (which "James") and date are debated; hold
them with appropriate confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for **wisdom aphorisms** and imperatives, vivid **everyday illustrations** (the
mirror, the bridle, the spark), **catchword** links between units, **diatribe**
("someone will say…"), the recurring concerns (**faith and works**, the **tongue**,
**rich and poor**, **patience**), and echoes of Jesus's teaching and **Old
Testament** wisdom and law (see [[core.intertextuality]]); **Greek** wording can
bear on meaning (see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in
a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- What practical wisdom concern is this unit pressing, and how do its illustrations work?
- How do honor, shame, and the situation of rich and poor illuminate the passage?
- How does the "faith and works" material function in James's own argument and setting?
- How does the passage echo Jesus's teaching or Old Testament wisdom?
- What would the original diaspora audience have assumed that a modern reader might miss?

## Common misreadings

- Forcing James into a single linear argument rather than reading linked wisdom units.
- Setting the "faith and works" passage against Paul without attending to what each author means by the terms in his own setting.
- Treating the aphorisms as detached slogans apart from their illustrations and concerns.
- Reading the rich/poor material without its honor-shame and social setting.

## Handling uncertainty

The identity of the author, the date, and the relationship of the "faith and works"
material to Paul are debated and read differently across traditions. Present the
major responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
