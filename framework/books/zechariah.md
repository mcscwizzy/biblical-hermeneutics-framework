## Purpose

This module helps you approach Zechariah as post-exilic prophecy that moves from
symbolic night-visions encouraging the temple rebuilding to later oracles about the
nations and a coming king. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells
you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It
does not tell you what Zechariah means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Zechariah, on top of [[genre.prophecy]] and —
because of its symbolic visions — [[genre.apocalyptic]].

## Genre signals

A **mixed** book. The first part is a series of symbolic **night-visions** with an
interpreting angel, anticipating **apocalyptic** ([[genre.apocalyptic]]); it also
contains **prophetic oracles** ([[genre.prophecy]]). The later chapters shift to
**oracles** ("burdens") about the nations and a coming king. Identify which mode
governs a given passage.

## Historical anchors

The **Persian-period restoration** ([[context.exile-and-restoration]]) — the
community rebuilding the **temple** ([[context.temple]]) under Zerubbabel and Joshua
the high priest — provides the setting for the early visions; the later chapters
range more widely. The unity and composition of the book are debated; hold them
with appropriate confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the sequence of **symbolic night-visions** and their **angelic
interpretation**, **sign-acts** (the crowning), recurring imagery (lampstand,
branch, measuring line), the later **"burden"** oracles, the **coming-king** and
shepherd passages, and the very heavy reuse of earlier prophets and influence on
later apocalyptic (see [[core.intertextuality]]). Do not assume every feature is
present in a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- Is this passage a symbolic vision, a sign-act, or an oracle — and how does that shape its claim?
- What does the symbol or image evoke, and how does the text itself interpret it?
- How do the early visions encourage the temple-rebuilding community?
- What major approaches exist to the later chapters and their coming-king imagery, and what are the trade-offs?
- How does the passage reuse earlier prophets, and how is it taken up later?

## Common misreadings

- Decoding the visions or later oracles as a coded timetable of modern events.
- Reading the whole book by one mode (all vision, or all plain oracle) rather than identifying the form.
- Settling the contested unity and composition by assumption rather than observation.
- Decoding individual symbols while missing the passage's overall message.

## Handling uncertainty

The book's unity and composition, the referents of its visions and later oracles,
and its coming-king passages are read very differently across traditions. Present
the major approaches, distinguish observation from reconstruction, and label
confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave conclusions to the reader.
