## Purpose

This module helps you approach Ephesians as a meditative, liturgical letter on the
cosmic scope of God's purpose and the unity of Jew and Gentile in one body. It is a
hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask, assuming
Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It does not tell you what Ephesians
means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Ephesians, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and the
Greco-Roman social world of [[context.greco-roman-world]] and [[context.roman-empire]].

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) with an unusually **liturgical, elevated** style
— long sentences, **blessing** and **prayer** sections, and **paraenesis** (ethical
exhortation), including a **household code** and the "armor of God." Its general,
non-situational tone has prompted debate over whether it was a circular letter.

## Historical anchors

The **Greco-Roman** urban world ([[context.greco-roman-world]],
[[context.roman-empire]]) — including the **household** structures and honor
relations behind the household code ([[context.honor-shame]]) — and the
**Jew–Gentile** unity that is the letter's theme. Whether Paul himself wrote
Ephesians is debated among scholars; present the major approaches and weigh the
evidence rather than assuming a verdict (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the opening **blessing** and **prayers**, the **long, layered sentences**,
the **"in Christ"** and "heavenly places" language, the **Jew–Gentile "one new
humanity"** theme, the **household code**, the **armor** imagery, echoes of **Old
Testament** language (see [[core.intertextuality]]), and **Greek** wording bearing
on meaning (see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in a
given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- How does this passage develop the letter's cosmic and Jew–Gentile-unity themes?
- How does the liturgical, prayerful style shape the way the passage communicates?
- How does the household code reflect, and how might it qualify, its social setting?
- What would the original audience have assumed about household, power, or unity that a modern reader might miss?
- How does the passage echo earlier Scripture?
- What does a debated term or construction mean in context, and what are the options?

## Common misreadings

- Reading the household code apart from its ancient social setting and the letter's wider argument.
- Treating the elevated, liturgical style as if it were tight forensic argument.
- Settling the authorship question by assumption rather than weighing the evidence.
- Lifting "armor of God" or "in the heavenly places" language out of the letter's flow.

## Handling uncertainty

The letter's authorship, destination (whether circular), and the application of the
household code are debated and read differently across traditions. Present the major
responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
