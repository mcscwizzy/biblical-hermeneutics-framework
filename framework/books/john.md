## Purpose

This module helps you approach John as the distinctive fourth Gospel — different in
style, structure, and vocabulary from the other three — built around signs,
discourses, and the question of Jesus's identity. It is a hermeneutic profile, not
a commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and
Context are already loaded. It does not tell you what John means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in John, on top of [[genre.gospel]] and the Jewish
world of [[context.second-temple-judaism]] — while attending to John's distinctive
idiom rather than reading it through the other Gospels.

## Genre signals

A **Gospel** ([[genre.gospel]]) — theological **narrative** ([[genre.narrative]]) —
but markedly distinct: organized around selected **signs** and long **discourses**
and dialogues rather than short sayings and many parables. Note its different
selection of events and its explicit statement of purpose. Read it on its own
terms, not as a variant of the Synoptics.

## Historical anchors

**Second Temple Judaism** ([[context.second-temple-judaism]]) — festivals, temple,
synagogue, and scriptural debate, all prominent in John — within the wider
**Greco-Roman world** ([[context.greco-roman-world]]) whose concepts John's
vocabulary sometimes engages. Authorship, date, setting, and the relationship to
the Synoptics are debated; hold them with appropriate confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **prologue** (the Word), the structured **sign**s, the **"I am"**
sayings, long **discourses** and dialogues, pervasive **dualism** (light/darkness,
above/below), **double meanings** and **misunderstanding**, **irony**, recurring
symbols (water, bread, light), festival settings, and dense scriptural and thematic
**allusion** (see [[core.intertextuality]]). Greek wording can bear on meaning
(see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every feature is present in a given
passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- How does this passage develop John's central concern with Jesus's identity and the response of belief?
- Is a term being used with a deliberate double meaning, and how does the misunderstanding motif work here?
- How does the sign or discourse function within John's distinctive structure?
- What Jewish festival, institution, or Scripture stands behind the passage?
- What would John's audience hear that a modern reader might miss?
- How does John's account differ from the Synoptics, and what does that emphasize?

## Common misreadings

- Flattening John into the Synoptics and erasing its distinct style, structure, and chronology.
- Reading a term at one level when John intends a deliberate double meaning.
- Pressing John's dualistic language into a system the text does not assert.
- Treating every narrated act as a direct command for readers today.

## Handling uncertainty

Authorship, date, setting, composition, and the relationship to the Synoptics are
debated and read differently across traditions. Present the range, separate
observation from reconstruction, and label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave conclusions to the reader.
