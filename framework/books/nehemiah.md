## Purpose

This module helps you approach Nehemiah as the narrative of rebuilding Jerusalem's
walls and re-forming the community — leadership, opposition, and covenant renewal
under Persian rule. It is a hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you
what to notice and ask, assuming Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It
does not tell you what Nehemiah means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Nehemiah, on top of [[genre.narrative]] and the
Persian-period restoration in [[context.exile-and-restoration]].

## Genre signals

Built largely around a first-person **memoir** ("the words of Nehemiah"), combined
with **narrative** ([[genre.narrative]]), **lists** and registers, and a major
**covenant-renewal** scene with the public reading of the Law. Read the memoir,
lists, and prayers each by their own conventions.

## Historical anchors

The **Persian-period restoration** ([[context.exile-and-restoration]]) —
rebuilding the city wall, resettlement, and social and economic reform under
imperial authority — with **covenant renewal** at the community's center
([[context.covenant]]). The chronology and its relation to Ezra are debated; hold
them with appropriate confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **first-person memoir** voice and its prayers, the **wall-building**
narrative and its **opposition** scenes, **lists and registers**, the **public
reading of the Law** and **covenant-renewal** ceremony, recurring themes
(prayer, leadership, reform), and the closing **reform** episodes. Do not assume
every feature is present in a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- How does this passage advance the rebuilding of the city and the re-forming of the community?
- What does the memoir voice and its prayers contribute to how the events are framed?
- Is the passage reporting events or commending a response?
- How does life under Persian authority shape the situation and the opposition?
- What is at stake in the social, economic, and boundary reforms, and what approaches exist to reading the sensitive material?
- What would the restoration audience have assumed that a modern reader might miss?

## Common misreadings

- Reducing the book to a leadership manual and missing its covenant-renewal and communal concerns.
- Skipping the lists and registers as filler rather than asking their function.
- Reading the sensitive separation and reform material through modern categories without engaging its setting and the range of approaches.
- Treating narrated actions as automatically endorsed or prescribed for today.

## Handling uncertainty

The chronology, the relation to Ezra, the composition and sources, and how to read
the reform and separation material are debated and, in places, sensitive. Present
the major approaches, distinguish observation from reconstruction, and label
confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave conclusions to the reader.
