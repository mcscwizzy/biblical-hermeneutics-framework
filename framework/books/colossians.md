## Purpose

This module helps you approach Colossians as a letter exalting the supremacy and
sufficiency of Christ against a "philosophy" that threatened to add to him. It is a
hermeneutic profile, not a commentary: it tells you what to notice and ask, assuming
Core, Genre, and Context are already loaded. It does not tell you what Colossians
means.

## When to apply

When interpreting any passage in Colossians, on top of [[genre.epistle]] and its
Greco-Roman religious setting ([[context.greco-roman-world]], [[context.roman-empire]]).

## Genre signals

An **epistle** ([[genre.epistle]]) combining a hymnic **Christ poem**, **polemic**
against a rival teaching, and **paraenesis** (ethical exhortation) including a
**household code**. Read the hymnic, polemical, and exhortation sections according
to their forms.

## Historical anchors

The **Greco-Roman religious world** ([[context.greco-roman-world]],
[[context.roman-empire]]) of Asia Minor, with its mix of cults, philosophy, and
ascetic and visionary practices behind the so-called "Colossian philosophy," and
the **household** structures behind the code ([[context.honor-shame]]). Whether Paul
himself wrote Colossians is debated among scholars; present the major approaches and
weigh the evidence rather than assuming a verdict (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).

## Literary features

Look for the **Christ hymn** (supremacy in creation and reconciliation), the
**polemic** against "philosophy," regulations, and visionary practice, **"fullness"**
and "in him" language, the **household code**, the **"put off / put on"** ethical
imagery, and echoes of **Old Testament** language (see [[core.intertextuality]]);
**Greek** wording can bear on meaning (see [[language.greek]]). Do not assume every
feature is present in a given passage; look for them.

## Key interpretive questions

Pose, do not answer:

- How does this passage assert or apply the supremacy and sufficiency of Christ?
- What can be reconstructed of the "philosophy" being countered, and how cautiously?
- How does the Christ hymn function in the letter's argument?
- How does the household code reflect, and how might it qualify, its social setting?
- What would the original audience have assumed about cosmic powers or ascetic practice that a modern reader might miss?
- What does a debated term or construction mean in context, and what are the options?

## Common misreadings

- Reconstructing the "Colossian philosophy" with more confidence than the evidence allows.
- Reading the household code apart from its ancient social setting and the letter's argument.
- Lifting the Christ hymn out of its polemical purpose.
- Settling the authorship question by assumption rather than weighing the evidence.

## Handling uncertainty

The letter's authorship, the precise nature of the "philosophy," and the application
of the household code are debated and read differently across traditions. Present
the major responsible interpretations and their basis, label confidence
(see [[core.epistemic-humility]]), and leave the conclusion to the reader.
