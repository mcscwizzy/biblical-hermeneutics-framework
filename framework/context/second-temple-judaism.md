## Purpose

This module helps you read the New Testament against the diverse Jewish world of
the Second Temple period (roughly the centuries between the Testaments and into
the first century CE). That world shaped the assumptions of Jesus, Paul, the
Gospels, Acts, and Revelation. The module is descriptive: it surveys the
landscape of beliefs and institutions, not which view is correct.

## When to apply

When interpreting New Testament texts that assume Jewish institutions, debates,
or expectations — synagogue scenes, disputes among groups, messianic language,
resurrection and afterlife, purity and Sabbath questions, and use of Scripture.

## Interpretive moves

1. **Recognize the diversity.** Second Temple Judaism included varied groups
   (such as Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, and others) with differing views; do
   not flatten it into a single uniform "Judaism."
2. **Place the text within live debates** of the era — for example, about
   resurrection, law observance, the Temple, and the calendar — without taking
   sides on the theology.
3. **Note messianic expectations were varied,** not singular; describe the range
   rather than assuming one fixed expectation.
4. **Attend to synagogue and Scripture-reading practices,** including how texts
   were read, translated, and interpreted in the period.
5. **Connect to Temple practice and purity** where relevant
   (see [[context.temple]]), and to the Roman setting
   (see [[context.roman-empire]]).
6. **Trace how a NT author uses Jewish Scripture and traditions**
   (see [[core.intertextuality]]) without assuming later or modern Jewish
   practice mirrors the first century.

## Common errors to avoid

- Treating Second Temple Judaism as monolithic, or projecting later rabbinic
  Judaism back onto the first century.
- Assuming a single, fixed messianic expectation.
- Caricaturing Jewish groups (e.g., as mere foils) rather than describing them.
- Asserting specifics about sects, dates, or practices beyond the evidence
  (see [[core.anti-hallucination]]).

## Handling uncertainty

Sources for the period are partial and the field is actively debated. Present the
range of reconstructions, separate well-attested practice from inference, and
label your confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]).
