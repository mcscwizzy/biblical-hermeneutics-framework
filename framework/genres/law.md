## Purpose

This module helps you read biblical law as covenant instruction embedded in a
narrative, not as a detached legal code. Much of Exodus through Deuteronomy mixes
commands, case law, ritual instruction, and story; meaning depends on the
covenant relationship the law serves and the ancient setting in which it was
given.

## When to apply

When interpreting legal, ritual, or covenant material — the Ten Words, case law
("if a person…, then…"), ritual and purity instruction, blessings and curses,
and covenant or treaty texts (largely in Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and
Deuteronomy).

## Interpretive moves

1. **Read law within its narrative frame.** Commands are given to a particular
   people at a point in the story; note who is addressed and what covenant
   moment they follow.
2. **Distinguish forms.** Apodictic commands ("you shall not…"), casuistic case
   law, ritual instruction, and covenant blessings/curses work differently; read
   each on its own terms.
3. **Ask the purpose behind a provision** — what relationship, value, or order it
   protects — rather than treating it as an isolated rule.
4. **Situate it in the ancient world.** Compare with ANE law collections and
   treaty forms (see [[context.ancient-near-east]], [[context.covenant]]) to hear
   what is shared and what is distinctive, without assuming borrowing.
5. **Track how later texts take up the law** before deciding how a command
   functions for later readers (see [[core.intertextuality]]); the question of
   continuity and application is contested and tradition-linked.

## Common errors to avoid

- Reading law as timeless legislation detached from its covenant and narrative
  setting.
- Flattening distinct forms (moral, civil, ritual) — categories that are
  themselves debated — into a single undifferentiated rulebook.
- Importing modern legal, political, or dietary assumptions into ancient
  provisions.
- Settling the question of a law's ongoing application by assumption rather than
  by careful reading and stated reasoning.

## Handling uncertainty

How biblical law relates to ANE parallels, how its provisions cohere, and how (or
whether) a given command applies to later readers are all debated and
tradition-linked. Present the major approaches, separate observation from
conclusion, and label your confidence (see [[core.epistemic-humility]]); leave
the conclusion to the reader.
