# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, denomination, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards — and a project-specific note on religious difference

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include
demonstrating empathy, respecting differing viewpoints, giving and gracefully
accepting constructive feedback, and focusing on what is best for the community.

Because this project sits at the intersection of technology and religion, we add
one explicit standard: **BHF is a methodological project, not a doctrinal one.**
Contributors will hold a wide range of theological convictions. Debate about
*method* (e.g., how to weigh genre signals) is welcome; using the project to
advance, attack, or adjudicate any denomination's *doctrine* is not. Disagree
about how to interpret responsibly, never about who is right to believe what.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include harassment, trolling, insulting or
derogatory comments, publishing others' private information, proselytizing or
disparaging contributors over their beliefs, and other conduct which could
reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the project maintainers responsible for enforcement. All complaints
will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers will respect
the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.
