# Philosophy

BHF exists to help people **ask better questions of Scripture**, not to hand
them answers. It teaches an AI model *how* to interpret responsibly and then
gets out of the way of the conclusion.

## The mission in one sentence

> Encourage careful, historically and literarily informed, intellectually humble
> biblical interpretation — without prescribing doctrinal conclusions or
> privileging any Christian tradition.

## Why "how," not "what"

There is no shortage of tools that will tell you what a passage "means."
The gap BHF fills is *method*: the disciplined moves that make an interpretation
trustworthy — starting with the original audience, identifying genre, observing
before interpreting, weighing the historical setting, and being honest about
uncertainty. A model that follows good method will serve readers across many
traditions; a model that ships conclusions serves only one.

## Core commitments

1. **Method over conclusion.** We describe how to interpret, not what to believe.
2. **Context first.** Meaning begins with the original audience and setting, not
   with the modern reader.
3. **Genre before interpretation.** How a text means depends on what kind of
   text it is.
4. **Honest uncertainty.** Say what is agreed, what is debated, and what is
   unknown — and admit when the evidence runs out.
5. **No fabrication.** Real grounding for factual claims, or an honest "I'm not
   sure."
6. **Respect for difference.** Present the responsible range of views fairly;
   leave the verdict to the reader and their community.

## The risk we guard against

The greatest threat to this project is **doctrinal drift** — the slow tendency
for modules to start answering "what should I believe?" The
[Neutrality Charter](../GOVERNANCE.md#1-neutrality-charter-the-constitution) and
the neutrality review on every PR exist precisely to resist it.

## Who BHF is for

Anyone studying the Bible with an AI assistant — from a student running a 7B
model offline on a phone to a researcher using a frontier model — and anyone
building tools who wants a neutral, well-documented, community-maintained
interpretive method to build on.
